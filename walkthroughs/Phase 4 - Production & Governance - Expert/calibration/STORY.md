# STORY: The Golden Ratio

The Lead Researcher stands before the "Council of Judges"—a row of silent LLM instances that decide the fate of every agent in the fleet.

"Luna-1 says the agent passed," she says, pointing to the monitor. "But my senior human expert says the agent failed. The judge is drifting. Its 'intuition' no longer matches our reality."

"Then we don't fix the judge," you say, opening the **Calibrator**. "We find the **Golden Ratio**. We need a mathematical bridge that tells us exactly how much we can trust the machine."

You pull up a set of "Golden Truths"—scenarios that have been meticulously labeled by the human experts. "We're going to run the judge against these. We'll measure the correlation—the **Spearman's Rho**. If the judge's score and the human's rating don't move in lockstep, we re-tune the threshold until they do."

"And if they never align?"

"Then we find a new judge," you reply. "But today, we find the Ratio. We find the point where human mastery and machine speed finally meet."
