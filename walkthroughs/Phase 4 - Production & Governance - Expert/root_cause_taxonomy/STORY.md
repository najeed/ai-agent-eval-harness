# STORY: The Forensic Heatmap

The data-room is dimly lit, the only light coming from a massive wall of monitors displaying the "Telecom Network Status." A sea of red icons covers the map of the Eastern Seaboard.

"It's not just one agent," the Case Officer says, pointing to the clusters of failures. "It's an epidemic. Different models, different tasks, but they're all failing in the same sector at the same time."

"Then it's not a logic error," you say, opening the **Forensic Toolkit**. "It's a systemic weakness. We need to stop looking at the individual failures and start looking at the **Patterns**."

You begin your investigation. One by one, you categorize the failures. 
*   "This one was a **Tool Misuse** in the high-voltage relay."
*   "This one was a **Reasoning Gap** during the fail-over sequence."
*   "This one was a **Domain Knowledge** failure."

As you map the root causes, a hidden "Heatmap" begins to emerge. You're not just finding out what went wrong—you're finding out where the entire fleet needs to be reinforced.
