# STORY: The Mirror Consistency

In the center of the **Mirror Room**, a single terminal reflects its screen into a thousand identical monitors. Each monitor is a shadow of the primary—an evaluation environment that must be a perfect twin of production.

"The results are coming back positive in the lab," the Security Architect says, squinting at a monitor. "But when we deploy the same agent to the staging mirror, he fails. The mirror is lying to us."

"The mirror isn't lying," you say, opening the **Parity Scanner**. "It's drifting. A single environment variable was changed in the lab. A different version of a library was installed in the staging mirror. To you, it looks the same. But to the agent, the reflection is distorted."

"How do we fix the mirror?"

"We don't fix the mirror," you reply. "We enforce **Consistency**. We run a parity check that compares every byte, every path, and every token. We find the **Drift** and neutralize it before a single trial is run. Because in this industry, a lie in the mirror is the most dangerous failure of all."
