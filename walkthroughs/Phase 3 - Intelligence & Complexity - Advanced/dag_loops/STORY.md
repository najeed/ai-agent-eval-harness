"He's been stuck for three hours," the Chief Architect says, looking at the blinking status lights of the Writer agent. "Every time he produces a draft, the Auditor finds a flaw and sends it back. It's a loop."

"It's not a loop," you say, opening the **Flow Monitor**. "It's a **Recursion Paradox**. In industrial writing, 'good enough' is a failure. The agent must refine, re-research, and re-write until the Auditor's precision threshold is met."

"But will he ever finish?"

"Only if the loop is grounded," you reply. "If we've designed the flow correctly, each turn through the recursion increases the probability of success. Today, you'll visualize the 'Paradox.' You'll watch an agent fail, return to the start, and prove that it can converge on a perfect answer through autonomous state persistence."
